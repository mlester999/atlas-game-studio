# Environment Asset Audit

Updated: 2026-06-30

## Placeholder Audit Summary

The authenticated town scene previously rendered the environment mostly with Phaser primitives instead of reusable game art. The main placeholder categories were:

- Ground and grass: full-world flat rectangle plus repeated diamond polygons.
- Stone paths and plaza: thick graphics strokes, circles, and small flat circles.
- Trees: trunk rectangles and overlapping circles.
- Buildings: generic polygon buildings with floating text labels.
- Portal and tower: primitive polygons, arcs, ellipses, and circles.
- Props: benches, flowers, market stalls, quest board, and lanterns drawn as rectangles/circles.
- Missing world richness: no reusable dock, boat, campfire, fences, signposts, stone props, crate clusters, or blacksmith exterior art.

## Replacement Asset Batch

New assets are generated by `scripts/generate-town-environment-assets.mjs` and stored under:

`apps/web/public/assets/soltower/environment/`

### Ground And Paths

- `ground/town-ground.png`: full 1600x1000 textured town ground layer with grass, flowers, water strip, cobbled paths, and plaza stones.
- `ground/grass-tile.png`: reusable 128x128 grass tile.
- `ground/cobble-path-tile.png`: reusable 128x64 cobble tile.

## Integration Notes

- `TownScene` preloads the new environment asset manifest and places sprites through `placeWorldObject`.
- The old world-sized flat rectangle and diamond tile field were removed.
- The old primitive path/plaza geometry was replaced by the baked `town-ground.png` layer.
- Major landmarks, buildings, props, trees, lamps, dock, boat, campfire, market stalls, and quest board now use image assets.
- Interaction targets now use per-object anchors and proximity ranges.
- Gameplay camera framing was preserved.
